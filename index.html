My Approach

How I Work

I blend Narrative Therapy and EMDR to support meaningful change, not by fixing you, but by helping you understand and shift the patterns, stories, and expectations shaping your life.

Narrative Therapy Working with the stories shaping your life.

We approach your life as rich and multi-layered. We look closely at the stories that have shaped you and how you see your life, many of which might have been inherited or imposed by others. Together, we work toward new stories that reflect a richer understanding of who you are and who you want to be.

Menopause & Midlife Supporting identity shift in midlife.

I'm Bria-certified and trained specifically to support the emotional and identity shifts that often emerge during midlife and menopause. This isn't about symptom management. It's about identity, grief, reclaiming authority, and navigating a profound threshold. We focus on reclaiming your authority, and making sense of this transition in a way that feels grounded, respectful, and meaningful.

EMDR Shifting the influence of past experiences on the present.

I'm EMDRIA-certified and use EMDR thoughtfully and collaboratively to help process experiences that remain emotionally or physically "stuck." This evidence-based work supports real movement and integration without requiring you to relive painful details endlessly.

Background

Meet Debi

I'm a licensed Marriage and Family Therapist with over a decade of clinical experience. My work is grounded in Narrative Therapy and EMDR, and shaped by a collaborative, non-pathologizing approach.

I work with people who are thoughtful, capable, and who are questioning ways of being that once made sense but no longer feel aligned. Together, we look at what has shaped your life, so you can move forward with greater steadiness and intention.

I also have advanced training in menopause-informed and midlife mental health, and support clients through identity shifts and life transitions that call for depth, context, and care.

COMMON QUESTIONS

A few things that often come up when considering therapy.

Do you offer a complimentary consultation?

Yes. I offer a complimentary 15-minute consultation. This is a brief conversation to ask questions, share what's bringing you in, and get a sense of how I work and whether this approach feels like a good fit.

Do you offer telehealth?

Yes. I offer telehealth sessions for clients located in California, as well as in-person sessions in El Segundo.

How can I tell if this approach is right for me?

This approach may be a good fit if you're looking for thoughtful, depth-oriented, collaborative therapy that makes room for meaning, context, and identity. Many people I work with are navigating transitions, questioning long-held roles or expectations, or wanting to understand themselves more fully.

A consultation can help you get a feel for whether this way of working resonates.

When might this not be the right fit?

This may not be the best fit if you're looking for highly structured, skills-based, or directive treatment, or if you're seeking immediate crisis support or intensive care. My work tends to be reflective, moving at a pace that allows for understanding and integration over time.
