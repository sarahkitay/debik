Common Questions

Do you accept insurance?

I am in-network with Aetna and Medicare. For other plans, I work as an out-of-network provider and collect fees at the time of service.

If you have a PPO plan, you may be eligible for reimbursement. I'm happy to provide a monthly superbill to submit to your insurance company. Reimbursement depends on your individual plan.

Please note that insurance reimbursement requires a mental health diagnosis. We can discuss this together and decide what feels appropriate.

If you plan to seek reimbursement, it can be helpful to ask your insurance company about out-of-network coverage, deductibles, and session limits.

What is your fee?

My fee is $250 per 50-minute session.

For clients using Aetna or Medicare, coverage and out-of-pocket costs depend on your specific plan. I'm happy to answer general questions during a consultation.

Do you offer in-person or virtual sessions?

Yes. I offer telehealth sessions for clients located in California, as well as in-person sessions in El Segundo.

How often do sessions happen, and for how long?

There is no predetermined timeline. Some people come to therapy around a specific concern or transition; others choose longer-term work.

We decide together how often to meet and for how long, based on what feels most useful. Many clients begin with regular sessions and adjust frequency over time, returning as new questions or life transitions arise.

Whether our work is brief or ongoing, it is grounded in meaning, values, and understanding.

Do you work with couples?

Yes. I work with couples who are interested in thoughtful, depth-oriented relationship work.

My approach is collaborative and non-pathologizing, and is often a good fit for couples navigating life transitions, identity shifts, communication breakdowns, or the impact of stress, trauma, or midlife change on their relationship.

Still have questions? I'm happy to talk through them.
